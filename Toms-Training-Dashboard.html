Knee Protocol
Bilateral Torn Meniscus · Left knee worse · No surgery · No swelling ✓
  • 🚫No deep knee flexion — stop at first pain increase. Most patients max out at 70–90°.
  • 🚫No full squats, long-stride lunges, heavy leg press or deep range leg work
  • 🚫No running on hard surface until Phase 3 gate is cleared
  • 🚫No twisting or pivoting movements under load
  • ✅LEFT knee is worse. All single-leg exercises: start RIGHT leg first, then match left. Left may lag 1–2 weeks — this is expected and correct.
  • 🌅Morning protocol — before getting out of bed: 5 min ankle pumps + quad sets. Reduces first-step pain significantly.
  • 💊Collagen protocol: 15g collagen + 50mg Vitamin C, 30–60 min BEFORE every gym and swim session. Vit C activates synthesis — don't skip it.
⚡ AM pain ≥ 5/10
Skip all leg loading. Upper body + swim only.
⚡ Pain rises during exercise
Stop that exercise immediately. Do not push through.
⚡ Pain after session
Ice 15 min, elevate, log it. If next day is worse → pull back 1 week.
✅ Goal every session
Finish with pain at the same level or lower than when you started.
🔵
Shoulder Protocol
Right Swimmer's Shoulder · Pull-phase pain · Subscapularis & biceps tendon
  • 🚫Never behind-the-head movements — lat pulldown, tricep extension, etc. Never.
  • 🚫No hand paddles — they amplify pull forces and make anterior shoulder pain worse
  • 🏊High elbow catch: fingertips enter water before elbow, elbow points outward. This is the single most important fix for your pull-phase pain.
  • 🏊Hand entry at shoulder width — don't cross the center line. Crossing causes internal rotation before the pull even starts.
  • ❄️Ice right shoulder 10–15 min after every swim for first 4 weeks.
